1. Overall Architecture Health
Rating: Poor

Key Problems Summary:

Service-layer boundaries are broken (UI concerns + store usage + mixed response shapes inside services).

API/IDB access is spread across services, stores, composables, and pages.

Pages directly call services and stores instead of going through composables.

Business logic is split between composables, pages, and services (not centralized in composables).

Sync/queue/data-write logic is duplicated across multiple layers.

Logging is ad-hoc (console.*) and not env-gated.

Several frontend files exceed the ~400-line split threshold.

Risk Level: High (offline sync correctness + long-term maintainability + regression risk during feature work)

2. Global Violations
Composable → Service direct calls (forbidden by your rules).

Page-level direct service calls (bypasses composable business layer).

Page-level direct store usage (UI layer coupled to state internals).

Store/composable direct IDB calls via src/utils/db (IDB not isolated to services).

Services not pure gateways (gasApi.js handles Quasar Notify/Loading; resourceRecords.js reads Pinia store).

Service responses not standardized to { success, data, error }.

No env-controlled logging strategy in services (process.env.ENABLE_LOGS-style control absent).

Oversized files (>400 lines) not split.

Naming convention drift in services (gasApi.js, resourceRecords.js, apiClient.js not XService naming pattern).

Finalize naming and cleanup
Enforce XService naming, remove deprecated files/shims, and update registries/docs.

Add targeted regression checks for login, resource fetch/sync, bulk upload, stock movement batch, and PR create/review flows before removing compatibility layers.
